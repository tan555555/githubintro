STYLES

Types of Dance A. Classical Dance Classical dance forms have a structured technique, often performed in theaters and accompanied by traditional music. Ballet (Europe) – A highly technical form emphasizing poise, balance, and storytelling. Bharatanatyam (India) – A spiritual dance form expressing stories through mudras (hand gestures) and facial expressions. Kathak (India) – A storytelling dance with fast spins and rhythmic footwork. Chinese Classical Dance – Incorporates acrobatics and flowing movements. B. Folk Dance Folk dances reflect the culture and traditions of different regions. Garba & Bhangra (India) – Energetic dances performed during festivals. Flamenco (Spain) – A passionate dance with guitar and handclaps. Irish Step Dance (Ireland) – Known for its rapid leg movements while keeping the upper body still. C. Modern & Street Dance Hip-Hop – Originated in the 1970s in the Bronx, featuring popping, locking, and breaking. Breakdancing – Acrobatics and power moves on the floor. Freestyle – Spontaneous dance without predefined steps. D. Ballroom & Social Dance Salsa – A fast-paced Latin dance with spins and hip movements. Tango – A dramatic and passionate dance from Argentina. Waltz – A slow, elegant ballroom dance. E. Contemporary & Fusion Dance Contemporary Dance – A mix of ballet and modern dance with fluid movements. Jazz Dance – Fast and energetic, often seen in Broadway shows. Bollywood Dance – A fusion of Indian classical, folk, and modern styles.
