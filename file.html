DANCING A THERAPY

Dance is a rhythmic movement of the body, often performed to music, as a form of self-expression, communication, or entertainment. It has been an integral part of human culture for centuries, appearing in rituals, celebrations, performances, and even therapy. 1. History & Evolution of Dance Dance has evolved over thousands of years, reflecting cultural traditions, social structures, and artistic movements. Ancient Dance (Prehistoric – 500 AD) Early humans used dance in rituals, storytelling, and celebrations. Cave paintings from around 9000 years ago depict dancing figures. Egyptian and Greek civilizations incorporated dance into religious and theatrical performances. Medieval & Renaissance Dance (500 – 1600 AD) In medieval Europe, dance was often performed at feasts and royal courts. Renaissance dances included courtly performances like the Waltz and Minuet. Classical & Folk Dance (1600 – 1900 AD) Classical ballet emerged in France and Italy, emphasizing grace and technique. Various folk dances like Flamenco (Spain), Kathak (India), and Irish step dancing developed. Modern & Contemporary Dance (1900 – Present) Jazz, hip-hop, and breakdancing evolved as street styles. Contemporary dance blends ballet and modern movements, focusing on storytelling. Dance has become mainstream with competitions, social media trends, and music videos.
